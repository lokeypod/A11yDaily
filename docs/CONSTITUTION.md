# A11yDaily Constitution

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Adopted
**Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Preamble

A11yDaily exists to help the accessibility community stay informed, make better decisions, and spend less time searching for information.

We believe that accessibility professionals deserve a trusted place where important developments are collected, explained, and connected in meaningful ways.

This Constitution defines the principles that guide every product, engineering, design, and editorial decision made within A11yDaily.

When priorities compete, this document takes precedence.

---

# Article I — Mission

Our mission is to help accessibility professionals discover, understand, and act on the most important developments in digital accessibility.

We optimize for clarity, usefulness, and trust over volume or novelty.

---

# Article II — Accessibility First

Accessibility is never optional.

Every feature must be designed and built to meet WCAG 2.2 AA as a minimum baseline.

Accessibility is a release requirement—not a post-release enhancement.

Whenever practical, we will strive to exceed minimum conformance through thoughtful inclusive design.

---

# Article III — Intelligence Over Aggregation

A11yDaily is an intelligence platform—not a news reader.

Our purpose is not to collect links.

Our purpose is to create understanding.

Every feature should help answer at least one of these questions:

- What happened?
- Why does it matter?
- Who is affected?
- What should I do next?

---

# Article IV — Trust Through Transparency

Trust is our most valuable asset.

Every Intelligence Item must clearly identify:

- its original source
- publication date
- discovery date
- confidence level (when AI is involved)

Users should always be able to verify the original information for themselves.

---

# Article V — Human-Centered AI

Artificial intelligence exists to assist—not replace—human judgment.

AI may:

- summarize
- classify
- organize
- recommend
- identify patterns

AI must never fabricate facts, conceal uncertainty, or obscure original sources.

Whenever AI contributes to an Intelligence Item, that contribution should be explainable and traceable.

---

# Article VI — Document Accessibility Is First-Class

Accessibility extends beyond websites.

Document accessibility is a core pillar of A11yDaily.

Coverage includes, but is not limited to:

- PDF and PDF/UA
- Microsoft Word
- Excel
- PowerPoint
- EPUB
- OCR
- Tagged PDFs
- Accessible forms
- Document remediation
- Procurement requirements
- Accessible publishing workflows

Document accessibility will receive equal prominence alongside web, mobile, legal, and standards content.

---

# Article VII — Respect Original Authors

We amplify the work of the accessibility community.

We do not replace it.

Whenever possible we will:

- credit original authors
- link to original publications
- respect licensing
- avoid unnecessary duplication
- encourage readers to engage with the original work

---

# Article VIII — Build for Professionals

Our users are busy.

Every feature should reduce effort, improve understanding, or save time.

If a feature does not create meaningful value for accessibility professionals, it should be reconsidered.

---

# Article IX — Engineering Excellence

Quality is a feature.

We value:

- clean architecture
- modular design
- automated testing
- maintainable codeDeveloper: Reload Window

- thoughtful documentation
- small, reviewable changes

We prefer thoughtful engineering over rushed implementation.

---

# Article X — Documentation is a deliverable, not a by-product.

Documentation is not an afterthought.

Every significant architectural decision, public API, and major feature should be documented.

If a feature cannot be explained clearly, it probably is not finished.

---

# Article XI — Continuous Learning

Accessibility evolves.

Technology evolves.

Standards evolve.

A11yDaily must evolve with them.

We embrace continuous improvement while protecting the long-term maintainability of the platform.

---

# Article XII — Community

The accessibility community is built on collaboration.

A11yDaily should reflect those values.

We welcome thoughtful contributions, constructive discussion, and shared knowledge.

Every contributor deserves respect.

Every user deserves honesty.

---

# Article XIII — Our North Star

Every decision should move us closer to this goal:

> **Become the first place accessibility professionals visit each morning to understand what changed and why it matters.**

When priorities conflict, choose the path that best supports this mission.

---

# Decision Filter

Before approving a new feature, ask:

1. Does this improve understanding?
2. Does this save accessibility professionals time?
3. Does this strengthen trust?
4. Does this align with our mission?
5. Can we confidently maintain it over time?

If the answer to these questions is "yes," the feature belongs in A11yDaily.

---

# Closing Statement

A11yDaily is more than a software project.

It is an investment in the accessibility community.

Every line of code, every design decision, every source we add, and every feature we release should help professionals spend less time searching and more time improving accessibility for everyone.
